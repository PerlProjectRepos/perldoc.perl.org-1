NAME

Pod::Checker, podchecker() - check pod documents for syntax errors

DESCRIPTION

podchecker will perform syntax checking of Perl5 POD format documentation.

Curious/ambitious users are welcome to propose additional features they wish to see in Pod::Checker and podchecker and verify that the checks are consistent with perlpod.

The following checks are currently performed:

  • Unknown '=xxxx' commands, unknown 'X<...>' interior-sequences, and unterminated interior sequences.

  • Check for proper balancing of http://www.w3.org/TR/xhtml1/DTD/xhtml1-transitional.dtd"> http://www.w3.org/1999/xhtml"> perltidy

    perltidy

    =begin and http://www.w3.org/TR/xhtml1/DTD/xhtml1-transitional.dtd"> http://www.w3.org/1999/xhtml"> perltidy

    perltidy

    =end . The contents of such a block are generally ignored, i.e. no syntax checks are performed.

  • Check for proper nesting and balancing of http://www.w3.org/TR/xhtml1/DTD/xhtml1-transitional.dtd"> http://www.w3.org/1999/xhtml"> perltidy

    perltidy

    =over , http://www.w3.org/TR/xhtml1/DTD/xhtml1-transitional.dtd"> http://www.w3.org/1999/xhtml"> perltidy

    perltidy

    =item and http://www.w3.org/TR/xhtml1/DTD/xhtml1-transitional.dtd"> http://www.w3.org/1999/xhtml"> perltidy

    perltidy

    =back .

  • Check for same nested interior-sequences (e.g. L<...L<...>...>).

  • Check for malformed or non-existing entities http://www.w3.org/TR/xhtml1/DTD/xhtml1-transitional.dtd"> http://www.w3.org/1999/xhtml"> perltidy

    perltidy

    E<...> .

  • Check for correct syntax of hyperlinks http://www.w3.org/TR/xhtml1/DTD/xhtml1-transitional.dtd"> http://www.w3.org/1999/xhtml"> perltidy

    perltidy

    L<...> . See perlpod for details.

  • Check for unresolved document-internal links. This check may also reveal misspelled links that seem to be internal links but should be links to something else.

Hyperlinks

There are some warnings with respect to malformed hyperlinks:

  • ignoring leading/trailing whitespace in link

    There is whitespace at the beginning or the end of the contents of L<...>.

  • (section) in '$page' deprecated

    There is a section detected in the page name of L<...>, e.g. http://www.w3.org/TR/xhtml1/DTD/xhtml1-transitional.dtd"> http://www.w3.org/1999/xhtml"> perltidy

    perltidy

    L<passwd(2)> . POD hyperlinks may point to POD documents only. Please write http://www.w3.org/TR/xhtml1/DTD/xhtml1-transitional.dtd"> http://www.w3.org/1999/xhtml"> perltidy

    perltidy

    C<passwd(2)> instead. Some formatters are able to expand this to appropriate code. For links to (builtin) functions, please say http://www.w3.org/TR/xhtml1/DTD/xhtml1-transitional.dtd"> http://www.w3.org/1999/xhtml"> perltidy

    perltidy

    L<perlfunc/mkdir> , without ().

  • alternative text/node '%s' contains non-escaped | or /

    The characters | and / are special in the L<...> context. Although the hyperlink parser does its best to determine which "/" is text and which is a delimiter in case of doubt, one ought to escape these literal characters like this:

    1.   /     E<sol>
    2.   |     E<verbar>

RETURN VALUE

podchecker returns the number of POD syntax errors found or -1 if there were no POD commands at all found in the file.
